About Me

I'm a UX and Service Designer at Cambridge University Press & Assessment, where I apply my passion for creating intuitive and meaningful experiences to educational products and services. My journey into design began in the classroom as a teacher, where I developed a deep understanding of user needs, iteration related to pain points, and the importance of clear communication. This background gave me a unique perspective on how people learn and interact with technology, which is something I bring to every project.

At Cambridge, I've had the opportunity to work on a diverse range of projects. I've designed experiences for AI machine learning products, including the world-leading IELTS English language test. Currently, I'm Lead Designer of the Global Exam Transformation project, where I'm focused on modernizing the entire internally and externally facing exam system, to create a more cost-effective, understandable and user-friendly approach for our global audience.

My work is driven by a commitment to working closely with teams to design solutions that are not only aesthetically pleasing but also genuinely useful and accessible. I love tackling complex problems and using a human-centered approach to transform them into simple, elegant designs.

I believe that great design is not just about aesthetics, but about deep understanding of user needs, rigorous testing, and iterative refinement. My expertise spans across user research, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, and design system implementation. When I'm not designing, you can find me racing triathlons or painting landscapes in a classical style.
