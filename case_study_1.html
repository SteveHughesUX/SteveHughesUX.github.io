[Case Study] Global Exam Transformation: Entries

A modern approach to the dated internal and external Entries examination system for Cambridge University Press & Assessment.

1. Project Overview

Problem Statement: Cambridge University Press & Assessment faced significant challenges with its aging examination system, impacting internal efficiency, user experience for many exam officers and candidates, and scalability. This project aimed to completely overhaul the system, introducing modern digital tools and processes to streamline operations and enhance the experience for all stakeholders globally.

Solution: A new back-end data Domain Driven Design architecture supporting new front end internal and external portals. Features include multiple validation points, improved usability and error prevention measures.

Timeline: The scope included everything from exam scheduling to all aspects of candidate registration. It is an ongoing multi-year agile initiative involving complex integrations and a diverse user base across different countries and languages.

My Role: UX researcher, UX Designer, Product Designer inc. user interviews, architecture design, design blueprinting, user journey mapping, lo-fi to hi-fi design and usability testing.

2. The Challenge

  • Outdated Systems: Reliance on legacy systems led to too many manual processes, data silos, and slow turnaround times.
  • Poor User Experience: Administrators struggled with complex interfaces, and candidates faced hurdles in accessing and retaining results information.
  • Scalability Issues: The existing infrastructure could not efficiently handle the growing volume of exams, users and requirements.
  • Global Consistency: Ensuring a consistent and equitable experience across OCR (UK) and Cambridge International (CI) markets was a major hurdle.
  • Security & Integrity: Maintaining high standards of exam security and integrity in a digital environment.

3. My Role & Team

Taking over as the Lead Designer from the previous designer, I led the user experience discovery and design strategy for all aspects of the new internal and external platforms. My responsibilities included:

  • Conducting extensive user research (interviews, surveys) with internal administrators and external exam officers.
  • Translating research insights into actionable design requirements and user stories.
  • Creating user flows, wireframes, and interactive Figma prototypes for various functionalities.
  • Leading usability testing sessions and iterating on designs based on feedback.
  • Collaborating closely with the product owner, front and back end engineers, and subject specialists.
  • Utilising and advancing the design system to ensure consistency and efficiency.
  • Advocating for user-centered design principles, accessibility and sustainability throughout the development lifecycle.

The product team: Product Owner, solution architect, back end specialist, front end specialist, subject matter expert, technical product owner, business analyst, business change lead.

4. Design Process

Our approach followed an Agile Lean UX methodology, emphasizing rapid iteration and frequent feedback.

Discovery & Research

We began by deeply understanding the current pain points and user needs through:

  • Stakeholder interviews with various internal colleagues.
  • User interviews with 30 exam officers (15 UK, 15 International).
  • Analysis of existing system data via presentations of the As-Is situation.
  • Service Design Blueprint based on information gathered.

Ideation & Design

Using our research, we moved into the solution space with ideation and design, focusing on intuitive workflows:

  • Collaborative workshops to make sure we were making the RIGHT THING, then MAKE IT RIGHT.
  • Creation of detailed user flows and information architecture.
  • Development of low-fidelity wireframes to test concepts using vibe coding.
  • High-fidelity prototyping in Figma using our evolving design system.

Testing & Iteration

Usability testing was continuous, allowing us to refine designs:

  • Regular usability testing with target users (remote and in-person).
  • A/B testing for key interface elements.
  • Gathering feedback from internal stakeholders and subject matter experts.
  • Iterating on designs based on quantitative and qualitative data.

5. Solutions & Key Features

The new platform introduced several key features designed to address the identified challenges:

Intuitive Exam Creation Workflow

A step-by-step guided process for content creators to build and manage exams, reducing errors and time spent.

Streamlined Candidate Registration

A simplified, mobile-first registration process for candidates, with clear progress indicators and error handling.

Centralized Administrator Dashboard

A comprehensive dashboard providing administrators with a holistic view of ongoing exams, candidate status, and performance analytics.

Secure Digital Proctoring Tools

Integrated tools for secure online proctoring, ensuring exam integrity and reducing the need for physical presence.

6. Impact & Outcomes

The new examination system delivered significant positive impacts:

  • Increased Efficiency: Reduced administrative time for exam setup by 30%.
  • Improved User Satisfaction: Candidate feedback scores on registration and exam experience increased by 25%.
  • Enhanced Scalability: The platform successfully supported a 50% increase in concurrent users during peak periods.
  • Reduced Errors: A 15% decrease in data entry errors for exam creation.
  • Global Adoption: Successful rollout across multiple regions with positive feedback from international teams.

7. Learnings & Next Steps

This project reinforced the importance of continuous user involvement and agile development in large-scale transformations. Key learnings included the necessity of robust localization strategies from the outset and the value of a strong design system for maintaining consistency across a complex product.

Future considerations involve exploring AI-powered adaptive testing features and further integrating analytics for personalized learning paths.
